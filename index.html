Our Mission

Manufacturing at the speed of the Americas.

Altus Energy exists to deliver utility-grade wire harnesses and cable assemblies from a single location — positioned to serve North, Central, and South American customers under the CAFTA-DR trade agreement.

Seven integrated production capabilities. One facility. Zero compromise. From raw materials to finished, tested assemblies — delivered with the speed, quality, and tariff advantage that define modern supply chain partners.

What We Build

Seven Capabilities.
One Facility.

01

Wire Processing

Automated cut, strip, and crimp on BOZWANG and equivalent high-speed machines. Precision lengths for complex multi-conductor assemblies.

02

Injection Molding

Twelve molding cells producing overmolded connectors, terminations, and environmental seals for utility and industrial assemblies.

03

Crimping

Reel-fed automated crimpers, PENNTEC pneumatic presses, and enclosed auto cells for consistent, validated crimp integrity.

04

Assembly

Schleuniger ultrasonic welding, JCWelec connectors, and precision gland-and-torque stations — assembled to engineering spec.

05

Auto Lines

Coiling, jumper production, and rotary indexing lines for high-volume assemblies requiring consistent takt time.

06

Quality Control

Dedicated QC with stop-line authority. Continuity, hi-pot, and pull-test validation on every assembly. Full drawing traceability.

07

Disconnect Boxes & Final Assembly

Scalable assembly benches where pre-crimped, pre-molded components are integrated into finished disconnect boxes, labeled, tested, and packaged — starting at four benches, scaling to eight.

Strategic Advantage

Your gateway to duty-free US access.

Under the Dominican Republic–Central America–United States Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA-DR), wire harnesses and cable assemblies manufactured in El Salvador enter the United States duty-free under HTS classification 8544 — provided the tariff shift rule is met.

Altus Energy's sourcing and manufacturing strategy is designed to qualify: raw materials from CAFTA-DR and NAFTA-origin suppliers, with substantial transformation performed in El Salvador. The result is a supply chain that is both cost-competitive and tariff-free into the largest consumer market in the Americas.
